title: Prestige mechanic (gaming)
---
A **prestige mechanic** (or simply, **prestige**, or **prestiging** as a verb) is when you reset a significant chunk of your progress to the start of a game. In incremental games, this is done with a more permanent benefit, and can sometimes span multiple layers. This was first introduced in [Call of Duty 4](https://www.ign.com/wikis/call-of-duty-4-modern-warfare/Prestige_Mode), where you gain a "prestige level" when you reset your rank back to 1.

## Multi-layered prestige
In incremental games, the prestige mechanic(s) often span multiple layers. For example:
- The **first prestige layer** resets the progress of the entire game, and say you get +10x Attack.
	- Prestiging again within this layer *does not* reset the +10x Attack. It still resets the whole game. If you get +10x Attack again, this will *stack*.
- The **second prestige layer** resets the progress of the layers above it.
	- So if you have +1000x Attack from the first prestige layer, that will also be reset. But you will usually be rewarded with more powerful upgrades.
Tedium-wise, prestiging in incremental games will make progressing through layers *faster* in each reset. For example, in [Antimatter Dimensions](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1399720/Antimatter_Dimensions/), you may spend a few days or weeks in the base game; but after a number of prestiges, you go through the base game (or the base layer) in a matter of seconds.
