The **UP student number** is a unique identifier for a UP student. For example, my own student number was `2018-12497`. It indicates I entered the UP system in the year 2018. We also largely refer to someone's seniority with the same four numbers. Referring to someone's batch by their *year of graduation* is uncommon, as (1) batch cohesion is typically present *during entry* and (2) graduation years are regularly inconsistent within the same batch.
## In UP culture

One does not easily disclose their *whole* student number. Some professors publicly publish grades, with students' full names obscured with their student number instead. In addition, there's a culture of mystery (and to a certain point, embarrassment) when someone finds out *you're old*, and that can be deduced from one's student number as well.
## Sequential numbers and campus-agnostic

The `12497` part is largely insignificant outside of identification purposes, but I do have a working theory that it is *sequential*, based on your UPCAT. My peers from high school are proximate to my own number, within the `12400`s or the `12500`s, as we largely submitted our forms as a batch.

There's an [anecdote on Reddit](https://www.reddit.com/r/peyups/comments/hppj2m/comment/fxt1570/?utm_source=share&utm_medium=web3x&utm_name=web3xcss&utm_term=1&utm_content=share_button) where two people have consecutive numbers based on their consecutive submission of their UPCAT forms.

The numbers are also campus-agnostic (the same set across all UP System campuses). However, this may change in the future and I can only guarantee its truth during my stay. I also cannot guarantee its truth in the past (say, in the 70s or the 80s).
